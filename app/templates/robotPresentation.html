PoseiTron is a coastgard-helper robot.

Its main tasks are to get the buoys back on the delimitation mark of the port's channels, redress the windsocks and light the lighthouse at night. At the end of the day, PoseiTron rises its flags and goes back to its rest area inside the port.

All of these actions refer to the 2020 edition of the rules for Eurobot competition.

Poseidon being the master of the seas and protector of the sailmen at the same time, it seemed a good idea to name our robot from him... because it's never nifty to get Poseidon angry.

PoseiTron is a differential drive robot with two free wheels for centered rotating point. These two driving wheels are powered by a 24V-motor each. Paired with encoders, odometers, buck-boost and on-board computer, it allows to drive the robot arround. Full system is relaying on a strong odometry, path planning and PI control loop all runing in the onboard computer and communicating through CAN bus with the electronic chip that directly controls the wheels.

In order to pick up the buoys, figurated by cups, PoseiTron is equiped with two sets of three pliers on each of its front and back sides. Each of these pliers embed a colour sensor to sort out the buoys and place them on the right side of the port channel. The pliers are actuated using pneumatic cylinders while the racks are driven up and down using Dynamixel motors.

Next two actions, caring for the windsocks to stay upright and ligthing up the lighthouse rely on the same system which simply consists of a pneumatic piston and then uses the displacement of the full robot to carry the action. Flags raising also uses pneumatic cylinders.

In order to avoid opponent's coastgard boats and more generally all kind of obastacles, PoseiTron uses ultrasonic sensors, fixed all over its two sides. This ensures redundancy in the signals and cover most of the important angles of vue for the robot.
